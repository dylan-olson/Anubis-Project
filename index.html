Welcome to Anubis Eternal Rest Services: A Journey Guided by the God of the Afterlife

Anubis was the ancient Egyptian god of embalming, cemeteries, and the sacred transition to the afterlife. Depicted with the black head of a jackal, he protected graves and oversaw mummification rituals to ensure the soul’s safe journey through death. The color black symbolized rebirth, linking Anubis to the fertile soil of the Nile and the promise of renewal.

Because jackals roamed near cemeteries, the Egyptians made Anubis their protector, hoping he would guard the dead from desecration. His role didn’t end with burial—according to The Book of the Dead, Anubis also weighed the hearts of the deceased against Ma’at’s feather of truth. A balanced heart meant entry into the afterlife; a heavy one was devoured by the monstrous Ammit, erasing the soul forever.

Rooted in the Egyptian belief in Ma’at—order, peace, and cosmic balance—Anubis served as the divine guide between life, death, and what lies beyond.

Mummification & Preservation

In ancient Egypt, Anubis was revered as the divine embalmer—the guardian of the dead and master of the sacred art of mummification. Believed to oversee the transition from life to the afterlife, Anubis played a central role in the preservation of the body, a crucial step for the soul’s journey through the Duat (underworld). Priests conducting embalming rituals would wear jackal-headed masks in his honor, invoking his presence to protect the deceased and perform rites with precision and purity. Mummification was not just a practical task—it was a sacred ceremony, and Anubis stood at the heart of it, guiding the hands of the living to prepare the dead for eternity.

Weighing of the Heart Ceremonies

At the heart of every soul's journey lies a moment of divine judgment—the Weighing of the Heart. In this sacred ceremony, overseen by Anubis himself, the heart of the departed is placed on a golden scale opposite the Feather of Ma’at, symbol of truth and balance. If the heart is lighter or equal in weight, the soul is granted passage to the eternal Field of Reeds. If not… well, the Devourer waits.
